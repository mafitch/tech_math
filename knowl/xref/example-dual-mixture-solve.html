Example 3.6.5.

We will solve the system from ExampleÂ 3.6.1. The two equations are
\begin{align*} A+B & = 10.0.\\ (0.91)A+(0.75)B & = 8.5. \end{align*}
Notice we can solve the first equation for \(B\text{,}\) then substitute it into the second. The result is a single equation with only one variable. We already know how to solve that one.
\begin{align*} B & = 10.0-A.\\ (0.91)A+(0.75)(10.0-A) & = 8.5.\\ (0.91)A+7.5-(0.75)A & = 8.5.\\ (0.16)A & = 1.0.\\ A & = \frac{1.0}{0.16}\\ & = 6.25. \end{align*}
Now that we know that \(A = 6.25\) we can substitute that into \(A+B=10.0.\) This gives us
\begin{align*} A+B & = 10.0.\\ 6.25+B & = 10.0.\\ B & = 3.75. \end{align*}
We can check that this works in the other equation (about percent alcohol).
\begin{align*} (0.91)A+(0.75)B & = \\ (0.91)(6.25)+(0.75)(3.75) & = \\ 5.6875+2.8125 & = 8.5. \end{align*}
