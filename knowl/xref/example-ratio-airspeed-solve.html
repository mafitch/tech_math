How long will it take to travel 236 nm? Round to tenths of an hour (a standard in aviation).

The proportion is
\begin{align*} \frac{110 \text{ nm}}{\text{hr}} \amp = \frac{236 \text{ nm}}{t \text{ hr}}.\\ \frac{110 \text{ nm}}{\text{hr}}(t \text{ hr}) \amp = \frac{236 \text{ nm}}{t \text{ hr}}(t \text{ hr}).\\ \end{align*}
Multiply both sides to eliminate the division.
\begin{align*} \frac{110 \text{ nm}}{\cancelhighlight{\text{hr}}}(t \cancelhighlight{\text{ hr}} ) \amp = \frac{236 \text{ nm}}{\cancelhighlight{t \text{ hr}}}\cancelhighlight{(t \text{ hr})}.\\ (110 \text{ nm})t \amp = (236 \text{ nm})\\ \frac{(110 \text{ nm})t}{(110 \text{ nm})} \amp = \frac{(236 \text{ nm})}{(110 \text{ nm})}\\ \frac{\cancelhighlight{(110 \text{ nm})}t}{\cancelhighlight{(110 \text{ nm})}} \amp = \frac{(236 \cancelhighlight{\text{ nm}})}{(110 \cancelhighlight{\text{ nm}})}\\ t \amp \approx 2.14545\\ t \amp \approx 2.1 \text{ hr}. \end{align*}
