Example 2.2.1.
Suppose we have a container with a solution that is 22% sugar and the rest water and another container with a solution that is 16% sugar and the rest water. If we combine 150 g of the first solution and 250 g of the second solution, what is the percent of sugar in the resulting solution?
Solution.
To calculate a percent we need the amount of the part and the total amount. We can calculate the total directly: \(T=150 \text{ g}+250 \text{ g}=400 \text{ g}\text{.}\) Note we know we can add these because both represented total amounts and they have the same units (g).
To calculate the part we need to know how much (rather than what percent) sugar was obtained from the two solutions. We can calculate that from the given percents and amounts.
\begin{align*} 150 \text{ g} \cdot 0.22 & = 33 \text{ g}.\\ 250 \text{ g} \cdot 0.16 & = 40 \text{ g}.\\ P & = 33 \text{ g}+40 \text{ g}\\ & = 73 \text{ g}. \end{align*}
Thus the percent of sugar in the mixture is \(P/T = \frac{73 \text{ g}}{400 \text{ g}} = 18.25\%\text{.}\)
