Example 6.4.1.

Solve \(6.0 = 10^{\frac{3}{2}(x+10.7)}\text{.}\)
Order of operations dictates the following
\begin{align*} 6.0 & = 10^{\frac{3}{2}(x+10.7)}.\\ \log(6.0) & = \log\left(10^{\frac{3}{2}(x+10.7)}\right). & \text{ log to undo exponential}\\ \log(6.0) & = \frac{3}{2}(x+10.7).\\ \frac{2}{3}\log(6.0) & = x+10.7. \text{ Multiplication to undo }\frac{3}{2} \cdot\\ \frac{2}{3}\log(6.0)-10.7 & = x. \text{ Subtraction to undo }+10.7 \cdot\\ -10.181 & \approx x. \end{align*}
Rounding here was arbitrarily chosen to be 3 decimal places, because there is no context.
