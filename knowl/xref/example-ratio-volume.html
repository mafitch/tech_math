Example 2.3.7.

Water is flowing out of a hose at a rate of 11 gallons per minute. How many gallons have come out after 2.7 minutes?
Solution.
We can set this up like the calculation in ExampleÂ 2.3.4. In that example multiplied the ratio (nm/hr) by the number of hours which gave us nm. Here we multiply the ratio (gal/min) by the number of minutes which will give us gallons.
\begin{align*} \text{Volume} & = \frac{11 \text{ gal}}{1 \text{ min}} \cdot 2.7 \text{ min}\\ \text{Volume} & = \frac{11 \text{ gal}}{1 \cancelhighlight{\text{ min}}} \cdot \frac{2.7 \cancelhighlight{\text{ min}}}{1}\\ & = 11 \cdot 2.7 \text{ gal}\\ & = 29.7 \text{ gal}\text{.} \end{align*}
