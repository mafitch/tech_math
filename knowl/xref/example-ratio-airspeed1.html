Example 2.3.4. Ratio: Airspeed.

The Diamond DA-20 cruises at the rate (speed) of
\begin{equation*} \frac{110 \text{ nautical miles}}{1 \text{ hour}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Cruise is a portion of flight in which the speed is typically constant. How far can the plane cruise in 2.5 hours?
The units suggest we can multiply these.
\begin{equation*} \frac{110 \text{ nm}}{1 \text{ hr}} \cdot 2.5 \text{ hr} = 275 \text{ nm}\text{.} \end{equation*}
This works because we are multiplying by hours and dividing by hours which leaves us with nautical miles as desired.
